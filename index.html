Here is what you cannot eat or drink

Be careful love ska pije me gaz

  • Coffee and Tea:

    Beverages like coffee and tea can interfere with iron absorption, so it's best to consume them in moderation, especially around mealtimes.

  • High Calcium Foods:

    Foods rich in calcium, such as dairy products like milk, cheese, and yogurt, can inhibit iron absorption. It's advisable to consume these at separate times from iron-rich foods.

  • Processed Foods:

    Highly processed foods like fast food, sugary snacks, and processed meats may not provide the necessary nutrients and can be low in iron.

  • Alcohol:

    Excessive alcohol consumption can lead to poor absorption of essential nutrients, including iron. Moderation is key to drink alcohol.

  • Uncooked Seafood and Undercooked Meat:

    Raw or undercooked seafood and meat can pose a risk of bacterial infection, which can be more problematic for individuals with weakened immune systems due to anemia.

  • High-Fiber Foods:

    While fiber is generally good for health, consuming very high-fiber foods in large quantities can sometimes interfere with the absorption of certain nutrients. It's about finding a balance.

Iron-Rich Foods

Vitamin C-Rich Foods

  • Citrus fruits (oranges, lemons, grapefruits)
  • Berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Bell peppers
  • Tomatoes

Folic Acid Sources

  • Leafy green vegetables (spinach, kale, collard greens)
  • Beans and lentils
  • Citrus fruits
  • Fortified grains and cereals

Nuts and Seeds

Almonds, cashews, and pumpkin seeds are good sources of iron and other essential nutrients.

Dried Fruits

Raisins, apricots, and prunes can be good sources of iron and can be added to cereals or eaten as snacks.

Fluids

Water is essential for overall health and can help prevent dehydration, which can worsen anemia.

Fruit juices, particularly those high in vitamin C content, can aid iron absorption.
